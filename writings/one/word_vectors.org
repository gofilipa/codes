** Queen = King - Man + Woman analysis
When word vectors were invented, its inventors introduced their power
to the world via a formula. The famous formula uses a
mathematical equation to calculate the meaning of the word "Queen":
#+begin_quote
Queen = King - Man + Woman (Mikolov et al. 2013)
#+end_quote
The meaning of the word "queen" is calculated by subtracting that of
"man" from "king," and adding "woman." This example, presumably chosen
for its intelligibility, speaks to how assumptions of gender are
implicated within those of patriarchy and imperialism. The meaning of
each term appears to be self-evident and universally true, stabilized
by the equals sign that balances the formula. (there are gendered
assumptions underpinning language, which go uncriticized, which are
even exemplified. This is why we need humanists in scientific spaces).
((Word vectors use gender norms as an operand, for the purpose of
doing math)).

The meaning of the word "Queen" is represented not by what the word
contains, but how it relates to other words in the database, like
"King" or "woman." Drawing from the concept of "vector" in math, which
describes coordinates for a line in graphical space, word vector
consists of numbers, a long list of numbers. For example, below is the
word vector for the word "queen":
#+begin_quote
'queen': -0.3583, -0.6767, 0.1689, -0.6164, -0.0135, -0.2284, 0.3359,
        0.1149, -0.1512, -0.8014
#+end_quote
Within the long list of probabilities, each individual probability
score represents that word's relationship to another word in the
dataset. One of these numbers may represent Queen's relationship to
the word "woman," and this number will have a higher probability score
than that which represents the relation to the word "man." These long
chains of numbers, which encode these probability scores between
"Queen" every other individual word in the database, function
effectively like the wordâ€™s definition. In kind of literalization of
the poststructuralist floating signifier, a single word is defined as,
not what it means in itself, but how it relates to (and differs from)
every single other word. In word vectors, then, meaning emerges from
association, from proximity and distance, between words.

The matrix format of the word vector enables a host of mathematical
operations and algorithms: from example, cosine similarity calculates
the distance between two vectors, that is, two words; and regression
analysis guesses the relationship between an independant and dependant
variable, or relation between one word and another.
